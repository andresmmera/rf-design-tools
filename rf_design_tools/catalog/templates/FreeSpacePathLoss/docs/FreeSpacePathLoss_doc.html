Free Space Path Loss

Free Space Path Loss Tool

The free space path loss is the attenuation between a transmitter and a receiver caused by the distance between them (d), without considering multipath or atmospheric absorption.

$$ P_{RX} = {P_{TX}} · {G_{TX}} · {G_{RX}} · {\left( \frac{\lambda}{4 · \pi · d}\right)^2} \tag{1} $$

The path loss is the difference (in dB) between the TX power (PTX) and the RX power (PTX)

$$ FSL (dB) = {20·log_{10} \left(d \right)} + {20·log_{10} (freq)} + {20·log_{10} \left(\frac{4 · \pi}{c}\right)} \tag{2} $$

Reference

[1] David M. Pozar, Microwave Engineering, 4th edition, page 673. John Wiley and Sons. 2012.
